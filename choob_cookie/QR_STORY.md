### A Sweet Legacy by Mana’s Grandma (Zahra)

When I was a young girl growing up in Tehran in the 1960s and 70s, my hands were always in flour. I baked bread for the neighborhood, planned celebrations for brides and their families, and turned every gathering into a memory. But there was one thing I kept close to my heart – a secret tea cookie recipe.

I would bake these cookies late at night, the scent filling our tiny kitchen, then wrap them in paper bags to give as parting gifts to family, friends, and anyone who visited our home. Every crumb carried love, every bite held a story.

When I moved to America, I brought that secret dough with me. My daughter grew up smelling the same warm aroma, and now she carries the tradition forward. She’s perfected it – a little more love, a pinch more patience.

These cookies were never about business. They were about **sharing love**, about making people feel warmth when they walked through our door, about giving a piece of our family to take home.

Now, we invite you to taste not just a cookie, but a piece of our family’s heart. Every bite is a hug from my kitchen to yours.
