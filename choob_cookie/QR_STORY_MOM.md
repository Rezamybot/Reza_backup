# The Choob Cookie Story

My grandma was the kind of woman who could do it all — baker, event planner, homemaker, bride and bridal party decorator — and she did it all with so much love and warmth that everyone who knew her felt it the moment they walked through her door. But her greatest magic lived in a small tin box on the kitchen counter, where she'd mix flour, sugar, and a secret blend of spices she never let anyone else see, rolling the dough into perfect little rounds and sprinkling black and white sesame seeds on top before sliding them into the oven. The smell would drift through the house, and soon neighbors and relatives would appear at our door, not just for a visit, but because they knew a paper bag with her special tea cookies — her way of saying "I love you, take a piece of my heart with you" — would be waiting for them when they left. When we moved to America, that tin came with us, and she kept baking, I kept watching, and I learned every movement by heart, and after she passed, I found myself standing in the same kitchen, doing the exact same things — mixing, rolling, sprinkling — realizing that the recipe was hers but the love in it had become mine. Now I make these cookies for my own family, and every time someone tries them, they tell me I should sell them, that they're that good, and maybe someday I will, but for now I just want to share a piece of our story with you because these cookies are more than a snack — they're a hug from my kitchen to yours, a tradition I want to keep spreading, one cookie at a time.
